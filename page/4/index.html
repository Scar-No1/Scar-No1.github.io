Reading Seminar: Sociology

This book is in the series of A very Short Introduction from Oxford. It generally tells about the consciousness of Sociology.

I’ve read the first chapter of this book. Its main topic is the position of Sociology.

The writer illustrates his opinion from two aspects. Firstly, writer compares the Sociology with th Science, he discusses about the characteristics of a good Science and the differences between them and Sociological characteristics. Specifically, it emphasizes that scientists are usually influenced by their interests and the opinion of value. It, however, doesn’t work in Sociology because Sociology need to be completely objective in order to analyse human behavior. Then, author discuss another problem that can Sociology become science. The answer is no. In Sociology, it is impossible to do experiment scientifically as there are too much varibles. Also, hardly can we collect accurate data from people’s mind.

What Sociology can do is to study the narrative details in order to get a valid conclusion. Sociology is not only to identify the laws of social behavior, it also requires us to understand.

Paragraph writing:My Biggest academic achievement

My biggest academic achievement

My biggest and most successful academic achievement happened during the last term of my studies at JingLing High School.

When I received my mid-term exam report, I felt a bit disappointed initially because of the bad score I got and I was very envious that other people could go home happily, with their report cards full of A*. It, however, didn’t discourage me. To the contrary, I became more motivated to improve myself. One day, I read an article about the importance of accumulation which really impressed me a lot, it said that accumulating some knowledge every day would have a great impact on you, so I decided to try it as part of my learning and revision strategies.

After school, I collected the notes for different subjects and reviewed the previous contents. I also tried to write down the question I met when learning and consult others to solve them all. These tasks gradually became my habit and I did them on a daily basis. A month later, I had sorted out a whole book of study notes and questions. This studying method was really useful and it helped me lay out a more solid foundation of knowledge. Compared with the past, my study time after school was better planned and managed, which make me feel satisfied. When the final exam came along, I still followed these strategies and carefully finished the daily tasks instead of doing the revision crazily until midnight. Unlike the other students, I didn’t feel too nervous because I knew I had prepared well . During the exam, I did my best just like I always do.

The result, in the end, was a bit unexpected, but in a sense it was predictable due to my improved study skills. I got first place in this final exam of this semester in my class which was a great achievement for me.

However, it should be noted that success will never be the reason why you stop working hard and the achievements can be extremely satisfying and rewarding but what can be as important is the good habits that you have developed and the harvest you get. From this successful experience, I am truly aware of the importance of knowledge accumulation. Just like the saying goes: “Constant dripping wears away a stone.” Undeniably, a drop of water has very weak strength which seems to be useless. It, however, will be powerful if the water continues to drop. The same is true of learning. Spending only 10 minutes to review the topic one day may seems useless, but if you keep doing this task every day, you will have much more extra reviewing time than others. Now, I always spend some time to take some notes to sort out what I have learned that day. What’s more, I also learned that confidence only comes from being well-prepared.

Though I made such a big academic achievement in this exam, there are more difficulties and challenges waiting for me. I should combine my success and failure experiences and learn from them to improve myself.

AI Competition

AI competition--feelings&harvests

In February this year, I signed up for the Haihua AI garbage sorting challenge. The word AI is far away from me from the technical level, but I can always feel its inextricable connection with our lives.
From knowing nothing ,to trying for the first time and to pursuing perfection, I have gone through countless studies and experiments. At the beginning of the project, we had a few AI classes. Mr. Chen Siyu explained the basic AI principles, such as linear regression of machine learning, separation and processing of structural images of artificial neural network, and gave us codes to teach us how to train and predict data. Although these theoretical knowledge are complicated and difficult to understand, and there are many unfamiliar terms to understand, they all lay a good foundation for the later model training.
The most experienced and fruitful part is the model training and data forecast upload after the team formation. When the teacher packs a training set into a group, the rest of the work would be done by ourselves. I tried to train by myself first, and soon learned that this task is extremely time-consuming. It takes nearly five hours to run an epoch on my laptop, which is far not enough. After teaming up with classmates, we began to try to run more programs on our own computers to train the model, but due to the equipment problems, all ended in failure. Failure is always the driving force to move forward, so we contacted TP’s teacher and provided a better performance computer there. After two times of TP debugging in person, plus daily remote control, continuous training, at the end of the competition, our senior group also had the fifth place, ACC 99.78.
Although I didn’t enter the top three as the senior two, I was very satisfied with my efforts, achievements and gains. Through this project, I have not only learned a lot about AI, python data analysis, but also learned that this technology is widely used in daily life. I admire those programmers who are careful enough to write a long and complicated code, and patient enough to debug parameters and wait for training results. Although computer is not my major, I know that there is no clear boundary between disciplines, and knowledge can be exchanged. This project has exercised my data analysis ability, and I have a deeper understanding of linear regression. AI technology can also play an important role in retail projects.

Paragraph writing:the biggest failure I experienced

This series of blogs are all stort paragraphs which record my daily experiences, feelings and thoughts. These can be used for the essay writing.

The biggest failure I experienced

Failure has happened to everyone, and sometimes they are caused by success.

The most painful experience I’ve ever had was at the end of my eighth grade when I came first in my final exam. I still clearly remember how ecstatic I was when I got the report card. I couldn’t wait to tell my classmates the good news and enjoy their admiration. I felt so proud of my achievement and felt like everything would come easily to me in the future. There was no doubt that I was overconfident.

Later that day, I was informed by my teacher to share the successful experiences at the parents’ meeting the following week. That was a surprising news for me, I had visions of professional speakers giving wonderful talks to enthralled audiences and I felt that was who I was; an experienced performer going to the stage calmly, having loud voice, clear logic and giving some creative ideas from time to time. So, in the following days before the parents’ meeting, I didn’t do any preparation. I kept telling myself that if I had got an A grade, then a speech would be simple.

But of course I would pay a heavy price for this arrogance and blind self- confidence and it became immediately apparent on stage that there was a gap between my ideals and reality. I finally realized a gap did exist between ideal and reality when standing on the stage. Suddenly, I had a blank mind and words failed me. Without any preparation, I couldn’t read the speech text directly so as to avoid embarrassment, I had to use some trite principles to fill the gaps in my mind. The speech was completely illogical and boring, with several seconds of silence when I couldn’t say a word. I felt awful. Seeing some parents sitting beside the stage becoming a little impatient, my self-confidence disappeared. My voice became smaller and smaller. I finished my speech in a hurry and ran out of the classroom without looking up. I felt ashamed and considered this as my biggest failure.

Success can be used as a driving force for people to move forward or as a reason for laziness. The lesson I have learnt from this humbling experience is that confidence is essential but overconfidence can lead to problems. As the saying goes, failure is the mother of success. Both failure and success should be faced accordingly on their own terms.
